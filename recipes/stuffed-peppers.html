Stuffed Peppers

Description

Stuffed peppers are a family favorite that can be easily customizable to your preferences

In this rendition, we will be using beef, garlic, onion, tomatoes, spinach, and rice to create a healthy, filling meal.

total time: 45 minutes

Gluten Free

Ingredients

  • 6 bell peppers, cut in half lengthwise and remove seeds
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves farlic, minced
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 15-ounce can diced tomatoes, drained
  • 1/2 cups of cooked rice
  • 1/2 tablespoon italian seasoning
  • 2 cups of baby spinach, roughly chopped
  • 1/2 cup of Montery Jack cheese
  • chopped parsley, for garnishing

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit
  2. Cut the bell peppers in half lengthwise and remove seeds and membrane. Drizzle with a small amount of olive oila nd rub both the inside and outside to coat them. Arrange them upside down in two baking pans or one half sheet pan and pre-bake for about 15 minutes, while you're making the stuffing
  3. In a large skillet over medium high heat, drizzle two tablespoons olive oil and saut√© the garlic and onions for one minute
  4. Add the ground beeg and cook until the beef is browned, breaking it up into smaller pieces as needed
  5. Drain off any excess fat/liquid from the beef and stir in the diced tomatoes, rice, spinach, italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Stir for a minute or two until the spinach is wilted (make sure you don't drain the beef liquid down the sink, this will harden and clog your sink)
  6. Remove the bell peppers from the oven and use tongs to flip them over
  7. Fill the peppers with the mixture and top off with a drizzle of cheese. Place them back in the oven and bake for 20 minutes, or until the peppers appear soft.
  8. Top with fresh parsley and serve.
